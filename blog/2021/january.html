Best Cricket World Cup Performances

Prakrit Baruah, January 2021

The debate over cricket’s greatest players is ever enduring. Pundits and enthusiasts alike wonder how Steve Smith or Virat Kohli would square up against Don Bradman or Sachin Tendulkar. Most dodge a definitive answer and say that players across generations shouldn’t be compared. Largely, I agree. Rules have changed, technology has evolved, and the game itself is fundamentally different now than it was fifty years ago.

However, this doesn’t mean that comparison is impossible. A player’s greatness may also be assessed in terms of how they hold up against their peers. Instead of using absolute measures of a player’s performance, such as the number of runs or wickets a player makes, we may look at the relative performance of a player compared to their contemporaries. The skill of a player is therefore their distance from the average player.

I focus particularly on the best cricket World Cup performances of all time. The following graphics show the greatest batting and bowling performances across the generations. I scraped the scorecards of every World Cup game since the event’s inception in 1975. Each player’s rank is determined by their z-score, a measure of the distance of a data point from the mean of its distribution. A z-score score of 3 indicates that a player is 3 standard deviations above the mean.

There are some notable conclusions. The batting rankings confirm Tendulkar’s brilliance: he shows up an astounding 3 times in the top 15 performances. Sri Lanka, Australia, and New Zealand are each well represented with three distinct players outscoring their peers. The same can’t be said for the bowling rankings. Australians dominate here with 4 out of 5 of the top bowling performances and an unbelievable overall 8 in the top 15.
